# Clean Summary

The session explained how Version 1 is approaching AI delivery and how that connects to the hackathon mindset.

Core message:

Version 1 wants to be known for helping customers use AI in a practical, adoptable, safe, and valuable way, not just by shipping flashy prototypes.

The AI incubator appears to focus on:

- delivering projects differently
- combining people and agents in delivery
- building internal AI capability and IP
- using partner platforms where helpful
- expanding AI solutions as a customer offering

The talk repeatedly emphasized that AI should start from a customer problem, not from the assumption that AI must be the answer.

# Key Positioning Themes

These were the clearest recurring themes from the session.

## 1. Make AI Work In The Business

Meaning:

- understand the customer problem first
- use co-creation and labs to uncover pain points
- decide whether AI is actually needed
- avoid forcing AI into the solution without a clear reason

Practical takeaway for the hackathon:

Start from a real user problem and be ready to explain why AI is appropriate.

## 2. Make People Want To Use It

Meaning:

- adoption matters as much as functionality
- tools alone are not enough
- customers need support in changing how they work
- change management and enablement are part of the solution

Practical takeaway for the hackathon:

Your demo should show why users would actually trust and use the product.

## 3. Build The AI Customers Experience

Meaning:

- build real AI solutions for customers
- use AI-accelerated delivery methods
- explore agent-based delivery models
- turn capability into customer-facing outcomes

Practical takeaway for the hackathon:

The project should look like a usable solution, not just a technical experiment.

## 4. Make It Safe

Meaning:

- governance matters
- security matters
- compliance matters
- standards alignment matters

Practical takeaway for the hackathon:

Even for an MVP, be ready to explain privacy, guardrails, approvals, or safe usage boundaries.
