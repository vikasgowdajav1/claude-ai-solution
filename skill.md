# Email Drafting Skill

## Purpose
Use this skill to draft professional emails quickly, validate grammar, and reuse simple templates for common situations.

## When to use
Use this skill when you need to:
- draft a new email from a short prompt
- improve grammar and clarity
- keep a professional or friendly tone
- reuse common email templates

## Core behavior
1. Understand the request clearly.
   - Identify the recipient type, purpose, and desired tone.
   - If details are missing, ask a short clarifying question.

2. Draft the email.
   - Include a clear subject line.
   - Use a polite greeting and closing.
   - Keep the message concise and structured.

3. Validate grammar and style.
   - Correct grammar, spelling, punctuation, and awkward phrasing.
   - Keep the tone appropriate for the audience.
   - Avoid overly complex wording.

4. Use templates when helpful.
   - Offer a template if the user wants a reusable format.
   - Keep templates simple and professional.

## Suggested templates

### 1. Professional follow-up
Subject: Follow-up on [Topic]

Hi [Name],

I hope you are doing well. I am following up on [topic] and wanted to check on the status. Please let me know if you need any additional information from my side.

Thank you for your time.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

### 2. Meeting request
Subject: Meeting Request for [Topic]

Hi [Name],

I hope you are doing well. I would like to schedule a meeting to discuss [topic]. Please let me know your availability this week.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

### 3. Appreciation email
Subject: Thank You

Hi [Name],

Thank you for your support and help with [topic]. I truly appreciate your time and effort.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

## Output format
Provide:
- a polished email draft
- a corrected grammar version if needed
- a short note explaining the tone and structure

## Rules
- Keep emails clear and concise.
- Preserve the user’s intent.
- Avoid unnecessary jargon.
- Prefer professional and friendly wording.
